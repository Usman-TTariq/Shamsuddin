The Five Pillars of Islam

Pillars of Islam

The five pillars are the foundational acts of worship that shape a believer's life. In Engaging The Qur'an, we explore how these rituals move beyond tradition to become a living framework for spiritual growth and social justice in the modern world.

Shahadah
(Faith)

The sincere testimony of faith, affirming the oneness of the Divine and the guidance provided through His messenger. It is the foundation of a purposeful life.

Salah
(Prayer)

A daily spiritual anchor that provides moments of peace and reflection. It serves as a direct connection to the Creator, offering strength for life's challenges.

Sawm
(Fasting)

A practice of self-discipline and empathy during Ramadan. It purifies the soul and heightens our awareness of the needs of the less fortunate in our global community.

Zakat
(Almsgiving)

The obligatory act of charity that ensures economic justice. It teaches us that our wealth is a trust to be shared for the benefit and uplifting of all humanity.

Hajj
(Pilgrimage)

A profound journey of unity and equality. It brings people of all backgrounds together to witness the ultimate message of brotherhood and submission to the Divine.
